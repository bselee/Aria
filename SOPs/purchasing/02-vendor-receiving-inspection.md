## Purpose
Inspect incoming vendor shipments for accuracy, damage, and quality before accepting them into inventory. Covers the physical receiving process at the dock or loading area.

## When to Use
Whenever a shipment arrives from a vendor or supplier and needs to be checked in against a purchase order.

## Risk if Not Followed
Damaged or incorrect products enter inventory, quantities get miscounted causing stock discrepancies, supplier claims are missed, and AP pays for goods not actually received.

## Sections

### Before the Truck Arrives
1. Check scheduled deliveries in Finale by reviewing committed POs with expected dates matching today.
2. Review PO details: item count, case quantities, pallet count, special handling notes (liquids, fragile, hazmat).
3. Clear the receiving area. Make sure there is space to stage pallets for counting and inspection.
4. Gather tools: clipboard, packing slip, barcode scanner (if available), pallet jack, utility knife, marker.

### Receive the Shipment
1. Get packing slip from driver, confirm PO number matches what you expect.
2. Count the number of pallets or packages before the driver leaves. If the count is short, note it on the delivery receipt and have the driver sign.
3. Move all packages to the receiving area. Do not mix with existing stock until inspection is complete.
4. Open each box or pallet and count every item individually — do not rely on case labels alone.
5. For each PO line item: verify correct SKU, correct quantity, correct unit of measure (each, case, pallet).
6. Check for visible damage: crushed boxes, torn bags, leaking containers, water damage. Photograph any damage immediately.
7. If a product has a lot number or expiration date: record it in Finale during receiving or on the packing slip for later entry.
8. For bulk soil or raw materials: visually confirm the product matches what was ordered. Note any off-color, unusual odor, or foreign material.

### Handle Discrepancies
1. If quantity is short: note the exact shortage on the packing slip. Do not adjust the PO quantity in Finale until confirmed with the supplier.
2. If product is damaged: photograph the damage from multiple angles. Set the damaged items aside in a marked area. Notify Purchasing via Slack with photos and the PO number.
3. If wrong product was sent: do not receive it into inventory. Contact Purchasing immediately via Slack with photos of what arrived vs what was expected.
4. If over-shipped: confirm with Purchasing whether to receive the extra or send it back. Do not accept over-shipments without approval.
5. For all discrepancies: add a note to the PO in Finale so AP has visibility.

### Stage for System Receiving
1. For accepted items: sort and stage them by SKU and location near their storage area.
2. Mark each pallet or tote with the PO number and date using a marker.
3. Signal to the inventory team (or proceed to) the Finale Inventory Receiving SOP to officially receive items into the system.
4. Do not put items away on shelves until the system receiving step is completed — inventory will be off until quantities are updated in Finale.

## Quality
- Every item is physically counted — never rely on box labels alone.
- All damage is photographed before the driver leaves the property.
- Discrepancies are reported to Purchasing within 1 hour of discovery.
- No items move to storage before the Finale receiving step is complete.
- Packing slips are saved and filed by PO number for audit reference.

## Cross-Department
- Soil Products: raw material deliveries may need a quality check by the Soil team — coordinate via Slack.
- Manufacturing: stage production materials near MFG area after receiving.
- AP needs discrepancy documentation to process vendor credits — always log and photograph.

## Troubleshooting
- Driver refuses to wait for full inspection: Sign for the pallet count only, note "subject to full inspection" on the delivery receipt. Inspect immediately.
- Item received but no PO exists: Set the item aside. Contact Purchasing to determine if it is a drop-ship, replacement, or unapproved shipment.
- Bulk material looks off-quality: Do not accept. Take photos, flag Purchasing and Soil Director in Slack, and hold for disposition.
- Supplier sends fewer pallets than expected: Note on BOL. Contact supplier and Purchasing immediately. Check if partial shipment was intentional.
